National Parks

Yellowstone, Without the Crowds

The trick to America's first national park isn't where you go — it's when.

Arrive at Old Faithful before 7 a.m. and you can hear the geyser breathe. Spend the late afternoon at Lamar Valley with a thermos of coffee — bison, sometimes wolves, and shadows that stretch a mile. Skip July. Pick early June or mid-September. Stay in Gardiner so you can slip into the park through the original stone arch before the day-trip buses wake up.

Pace: 4 nights, gentle walking. Best photo: Grand Prismatic from the overlook trail at golden hour.

Small-Town America

Charleston Side Streets

Forget the carriage tours — the city's real personality is on its quieter blocks.

Walk Tradd Street at dawn, when the gas lamps are still flickering and the only sound is a sweetgrass weaver setting up. Brunch at a Wagener Terrace bakery, then drift to Hampton Park for a bench under the live oaks. The Battery is famous; Bee Street and Cannonborough are the part you'll write home about.

Pace: 3 nights, walkable. Try: a sunset porch hour at a 19th-century B&B.

Cruises

Why Alaska Is the Cruise to Pick First

Glacier days, salmon dinners, and a deck chair pointed at silence.

An Inside Passage sailing gives you the gentlest water on the continent, plus a cabin that doesn't move much at night. Pick a smaller ship if you can — under 1,000 guests means closer glacier viewing and simpler boarding. Pack one warm layer more than you think you need; the wind off Hubbard Glacier makes its own opinion.

Best months: late May, early September. Skip: July weekends — every port crowds at once.

Bucket List

Paris, the Second-Visit Way

Already saw the Eiffel Tower? Good — now the city actually opens up.

Rent an apartment in the 11th, not a hotel near the Champs. Take morning walks along Canal Saint-Martin, lunch at a workers' bistro with a chalkboard menu, and one slow afternoon at the Musée Marmottan instead of the Louvre. Day trip by train to Reims for cathedrals and champagne cellars — both grandfathered into the price of a glass.

Pace: 7 nights, train-friendly. Bring: good shoes; cobblestones are unforgiving.

National Parks

Glacier in Wildflower Season

Two weeks each year the meadows show off. This is how to catch them.

Mid-July to early August, the Logan Pass meadows turn into a quilt — bear grass, paintbrush, glacier lily. Drive Going-to-the-Sun Road east to west in the morning so the light hits the cliffs as you climb. Hidden Lake Overlook is short, mostly boardwalk, and the most rewarding mile of trail in the park.

Stay: a lake-side cabin in East Glacier. Tip: book a red bus tour for the day you don't want to drive.

Small-Town America

Vermont in October — Slow Loop

A four-day driving loop with no highway, no rush, and a lot of cider.

Start in Woodstock, drift north through Quechee, on to Stowe, then back through Waterbury. Roads 100 and 12 are the prettiest in America the second week of October. Stay at family-run inns that still leave a thermos by your door. Lunch at a covered bridge, dinner at whichever place has a wood stove going.

Pace: 4 nights, easy driving. Try: a fall foliage rail tour from White River Junction.
