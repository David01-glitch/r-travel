About Retire & Roam

We are a small, independent publication writing for people in the best decade of their travel life — when there is finally time to do it slowly, and the wisdom to do it well.

Say hello
📍 Acadia, Maine

Why we exist

Most travel content online is built to sell something — a tour, a hotel, a credit card. We wanted a place that just shows. A photo essay. A short story about an unhurried morning. A specific bench in a specific town that we think you would love.

What we cover

  • National parks — at the right hour, in the right month, on the right trail for a relaxed pace.
  • Small-town America — Main streets, county fairs, B&Bs that remember your coffee order.
  • Cruises & rivers — when one suitcase is plenty and the view changes for you.
  • Bucket-list destinations — the big trips, planned to feel achievable, not exhausting.

How we make money

We are supported by tasteful display advertising and the occasional reader contribution. We are not paid by hotels, cruise lines, or tour operators to feature them. If we ever accept a hosted stay, we will say so directly inside the article.

Our promise

  • We will write honestly about pace, accessibility, and what a place really feels like.
  • We will not pretend a place is empty when it isn't.
  • We will keep this site simple, fast, and free of pop-ups that fight you.

Behind the byline

Retire & Roam is edited from a small home office on the East Coast of the United States. Our writers are former teachers, journalists, and park volunteers who travel year-round. If you have a place you love, we would like to hear about it — write to us at Retiretravel0034@gmail.com.
